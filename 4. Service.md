# Service

A means of enabling **value co-creation** by facilitating **outcomes** that customers want to achieve, without the customer having to manage specific **costs** and **risks**.

## Key Concepts

### What is Value?

The **perceived benefits, usefulness, and importance** of something.

**Key Points:**

- Value is subjective - based on customer perceptions and business outcomes
- True value is achieved through active collaboration between providers and consumers
- Value = Utility + Warranty

### What is an Outcome?

A **result for a stakeholder** enabled by one or more outputs.

**Definition:** A tangible or intangible deliverable that occurs by carrying out an activity and contributes to achieving an objective.

**Example:** Improved customer satisfaction (outcome) from faster response times (output).

### What is Cost?

The **amount of money spent** on a specific activity or resource.

**Key Points:**

- Can be expressed in non-monetary terms (time, people, effort)
- Customers want to avoid managing these costs directly

**Example:** Server maintenance costs, staff training expenses.

### What is Risk?

A **possible event** that could cause harm, loss, or make it more difficult to achieve objectives.

**Key Points:**

- Consumers can help reduce risk by communicating requirements and constraints clearly
- Services help customers avoid managing specific risks

**Example:** Data security risks, system downtime risks.

## Value Co-Creation

### Components of Value

Value cannot be created without both:

**Utility** - The functionality offered by a product or service to meet a particular need.

- **"Fit for purpose"**
- What the service does
- Removes constraints from the customer

**Warranty** - Assurance that a product or service will meet agreed requirements.

- **"Fit for use"**
- How well the service performs
- Provides assurance about availability, capacity, security, continuity

**Example:** Email service utility = sending/receiving messages; warranty = 99.9% uptime, secure transmission.

## Service-Related Definitions

### What is a Product?

A **configuration of an organization's resources** designed to offer value for a consumer.

**Key Points:**

- Can be used by one or more service offerings
- Combines resources to deliver specific value

**Example:** A backup solution product that includes software, storage, and support.

### What is a Resource?

**Something you own or have access to** that can be used to create value.

**Types of Resources:**

- People
- Information and knowledge
- Technology and infrastructure
- Value streams and processes
- Suppliers and partners

### What is a Service Offering?

A **formal description of one or more services**, designed to address the needs of a target consumer group.

## Components of Service Offerings

A service offering may include:

### 1. Goods

**Something transferred from supplier to consumer** - consumer takes ownership.

**Examples:**

- Phone
- Tablet
- Laptop
- Software licenses

### 2. Access to Resources

**Something granted or licensed** to the consumer under specific terms - ownership remains with provider.

**Examples:**

- Data center access
- Cloud services (AWS, Azure)
- Internet connectivity
- Shared applications

### 3. Service Actions

**Actions performed by the provider** to support consumer needs.

**Examples:**

- Help desk support
- Consulting services
- Training and education
- Maintenance and repairs

**Note:** Not all components are required - depends on the specific service and customer needs.

## What is Service Management?

A set of **specialized organizational capabilities** for enabling value for customers in the form of services.

**Purpose:** To help organizations deliver services that create value for customers while managing costs and risks effectively.
