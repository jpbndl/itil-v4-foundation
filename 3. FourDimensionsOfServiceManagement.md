# Four Dimensions of Service Management

The four **perspectives** that are critical to the effective and efficient facilitation of value for customers and other stakeholders in the form of products and services.

## The Four Dimensions

### 1. Organizations and People

Focuses on **human aspects** of service management.

**Key Elements:**

- Roles and responsibilities
- Collaboration culture based on shared values
- Staffing, skills, and competencies
- Governance and management
- People's knowledge and capabilities
- Communication and leadership

**Example:** Having trained support staff with clear roles and good teamwork skills.

### 2. Information and Technology

All **information and technology** used to provision and consume services.

**Key Elements:**
- Data and related security
- Workflow management systems
- Knowledge bases and documentation
- Applications and infrastructure
- Development tools and technologies
- Information architecture

**Example:** Using a ticketing system to track incidents and maintain a knowledge base for solutions.

### 3. Partners and Suppliers

Managing **third-party relationships** and the overall level of formality required.

**Key Elements:**

- Supplier management and contracts
- Partnership agreements
- Service integration and management
- Risk sharing and collaboration
- Vendor relationships
- Outsourcing arrangements

**Example:** Working with a cloud provider for hosting services or hardware vendor for equipment.

### 4. Value Streams and Processes

Defines **what is needed to achieve objectives** through structured activities.

**Key Elements:**

- Activities and workflows
- Controls and procedures
- Process design and optimization
- Value stream mapping
- Performance measurement
- Continuous improvement

**Example:** Having a defined process for handling customer requests from initial contact to resolution.

## External Factors (PESTLE)

Every dimension is affected by multiple **external factors:**

- **Political** - Government policies, regulations, political stability
- **Economic** - Market conditions, economic trends, budget constraints
- **Social** - Cultural factors, demographics, social trends
- **Technological** - Technology advances, automation, digital transformation
- **Legal** - Laws, regulations, compliance requirements
- **Environmental** - Sustainability, climate change, environmental regulations

**Example:** GDPR (Legal factor) affects how customer data is handled across all four dimensions.
