# Service Value System (SVS)

A model representing how all components and activities of an organization work together to **facilitate value creation**.

## Five Key Components

### 1. Guiding Principles
Seven principles that guide decision-making and behavior in all circumstances.

### 2. Governance
The means by which an organization is directed and controlled through policies and regulations.

1. Focus on Value
2. Start Where You Are
3. Progress Iteratively with Feedback
4. Collaborate and Promote Visibility
5. Think and Work Holistically
6. Keep it Simple and Practical
7. Optimize and Automate

**Role of governance in SVS:**
- Should be driven from top-down with parts delegated as needed
- A governing body is accountable for an organization's compliance
- Governing body needs to retain oversight

### 3. Service Value Chain
A model for service providers that covers all the key activities required to effectively manage products and services.

**Six Value Chain Activities:**
1. **Plan** - Ensure shared understanding of vision, status, and improvement direction
2. **Engage** - Provide transparency and good relationships with stakeholders
3. **Design and Transition** - Ensure products and services meet stakeholder expectations
4. **Obtain/Build** - Ensure service components are available when needed
5. **Deliver and Support** - Ensure services are delivered according to specifications
6. **Improve** - Ensure continual improvement of products, services, and practices

### 4. Practices
A set of organizational resources designed for performing work or accomplishing an objective.

**Three categories of practices (34 total):**
- **General Management Practices** - Adapted from general business management
- **Service Management Practices** - Developed specifically for service management
- **Technical Management Practices** - Adapted from technology management

### 5. Continual Improvement
A recurring organizational activity performed at all levels to ensure performance meets stakeholder expectations.

**Includes:**
- The ITIL Continual Improvement model
- The Improve activity (from Service Value Chain)
- The Continual Improvement practice

## Inputs and Outputs

### Inputs
**Opportunity and Demand** drive the SVS:
- **Opportunity** - Options to add value for stakeholders
- **Demand** - Need or desire for products and services

**Examples:**
- Add value to an existing service for stakeholders
- Provide a new service and create new value for stakeholders

### Output
**Value** is the primary output of SVS.

**Value Definition:** The perceived benefits, usefulness, and importance of something, based on outcomes the stakeholder needs.

## Determining Value

**How to know what is valuable?**
Through **communication, collaboration, and working together** with all stakeholders to understand their needs and expectations.

## Key Takeaway

The SVS ensures that all organizational components work together systematically to create value for stakeholders through coordinated activities, guided by principles, and governed by appropriate oversight.
